Net-Zero Office Building

Viridi net-zero office building

    Viridi’s net-zero energy office building is a structure composed primarily of mass timber systems, with a concrete foundation and elevator shafts. Mass timber is selected for its low carbon impact, ease of on-site assembly, and aesthetic qualities. Cross laminated timber (CLT) panels will be the structural basis for walls, floors, and roofing. Beams and columns will be glulam mass timber units. The building’s foundation slab will be poured concrete. Elevator shafts are concrete for additional fire safety. Dimension wood cladding is specified.

HISTORIC SITE RECORDING

York Courtyard site recording

The York Court is a shared outdoor space for buildings found on 527-531 Sussex Drive, Ottawa. It is a section of the connecting courtyards found in the BywardMarket which include Clarendon and Jeanne D’ Arc Courts.

    The project
  • The geographic location of the site has been identified using ArcGIS.

  • Main corners of the courtyard are mapped using a total station. Points then transferred in to AutoCAD to design the floor area.

  • Rectifying photography is then used to record the flat structures of the building facade.

  • 3D laser scanner is then implemented to design elevation


Professional engineering report has been written to explain the importance of heritage site recording in order to preserve character defining elements.

Earphone Case

Reverse Engineering project

    The purpose of this report is to design an earphone case that is more efficient in many ways. Even though earphone have been used for many years, there are still technical and design flaws in the wide variety of earphone cases. In this project, my teammate and I have redesigned an earphone case with some improvements. The main improvements that have been applied to this design are: minimizing the time that takes users to wrap their earphones properly and maximizing its mobility, so that it can be carried in user’s pocket everywhere.

    Steps taken to bring the design into tangible object

  • 2D design of the project is done using Auto CAD and Intelli CAD

  • 3D design is created by using Creo Parametric

  • The final design is then printed by 3D printer


Professional engineering report has been written explaining failure modes and benefits of the design.
